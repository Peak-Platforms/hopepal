HopePal — Ministry Operator Guide

From Raw Doc to Live Lesson
in Nine Steps

Everything you need to take a weekly teaching and put it in front of your drivers — no technical knowledge required.

1. Format your doc 2. Generate audio 3. Upload to Drive 4. Open admin 5. Fill in fields 6. Paste Drive link 7. Preview audio 8. Publish 9. Test
1
Format your doc using the template
Takes 10–20 minutes per teaching
Why this matters
The audio quality from NotebookLM depends almost entirely on how well your doc is structured. A plain document produces a generic conversation. A well-structured document produces an engaging, specific podcast your drivers will actually listen to.
The template — copy this structure into every doc
WEEK [NUMBER]: [TITLE] Scripture Book Chapter:Verse — "Full verse text" The Big Question One sentence framed as a question the whole teaching answers. Make it specific to OTR life. The Tension A common belief or struggle this teaching pushes back on. One paragraph. Reference driving life. The Teaching 3–5 paragraphs in plain conversational language. Include at least one story and one reference to OTR life. Key Verse (Repeat) Restate the scripture in one sentence summary. The Turn The moment the teaching shifts from problem to hope. One paragraph. Road Application 3 practical things a driver can do this week. Write as full sentences, not a list. Conversation Starters 3 questions the podcast hosts can debate. These directly shape the audio quality. Closing Thought One or two sentences. The thing a driver is still thinking about 200 miles later.
💡 Most important sections: The Tension and Conversation Starters. These two sections determine how engaging the podcast conversation will be. Write them first.
2
Generate the audio in NotebookLM
Free — produces a two-host podcast conversation
  • 1 Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with your Google account
  • 2 Click New Notebook
  • 3 Click Add Source → upload your formatted doc
  • 4 On the right panel find Audio Overview → click Customize
  • 5 Paste the prompt below into the instructions field
  • 6 Click Generate — takes 2–3 minutes
  • 7 When complete, click the ⋮ menu → Download — saves as a .m4a file
Paste this prompt into the Customize field
Create an Audio Overview as a warm, conversational two-host podcast for long-haul truck drivers. Open with THE BIG QUESTION as the hook. Work through THE TENSION with honest back-and-forth. Use the stories in THE TEACHING as talking points — react naturally. Spend real time on ROAD APPLICATION — make it practical. Close on the CLOSING THOUGHT with sincerity. Tone: warm, honest, occasionally funny, always hopeful. Length: 8 to 12 minutes. Audience: men and women who spend weeks away from family, live in a cab, and are looking for something real — not polished religion.
⚠️ Name the downloaded file using this format: week-01-title.m4a — this keeps lessons in order when you manage them later.
3
Upload to Google Drive and get a share link
Takes 2 minutes
  • 1 Go to drive.google.com
  • 2 Drag and drop the .m4a file into Drive, or click New → File upload
  • 3 Once uploaded, right-click the file → Share
  • 4 Under "General access" change to Anyone with the link
  • 5 Make sure the role is set to Viewer
  • 6 Click Copy link → keep this copied, you'll need it in Step 6
💡 Create a folder in Google Drive called HopePal Lessons and keep all your .m4a files there. Easier to find and manage as the library grows.
4
Open the admin page
Your lesson management dashboard
Open your browser and go to:
https://hopepal.life/admin
You will see a sidebar on the left with all 52 weeks grouped by topic. A green dot next to a week means it is already published and live. A dim dot means it is still a draft.
🔐 Keep this URL private — it gives full access to edit and publish lessons.
5
Select the week and fill in the fields
Title, scripture, summary, and keywords
  • 1 Click the week number in the sidebar that matches your teaching
  • 2 The editor opens on the right with the current placeholder content
  • 3 Replace Title with your actual teaching title
  • 4 Replace Scripture with the primary verse — format: Jeremiah 29:11
  • 5 Write a Summary — 1 to 2 plain sentences describing what the teaching is about
  • 6 Add Keywords — type each word and press Enter. Think about what a driver would actually say or type when they need this teaching. Examples: lonely, wife, argument, 3am, kids, fired, afraid
  • 7 Add Themes — broader categories like identity, marriage, faith, purpose
💡 Keywords are the most important field. The chatbot matches driver queries against keywords first. The more specific and emotion-based your keywords, the better the match. Think feelings, not theology.
6
Paste your Google Drive link
The admin page converts it automatically
  • 1 Scroll down to the Audio section in the editor
  • 2 Paste your Google Drive share link into the field
  • 3 A green confirmation message appears: "Google Drive link converted"
  • 4 The converted playback URL appears below it automatically
  • 5 Enter the Duration in minutes — check the .m4a file length in Google Drive
⚠️ If you see a red error message instead of green, your Drive sharing is not set to "Anyone with the link." Go back to Drive and check the share settings.
7
Preview the audio
Confirm it plays before publishing
  • 1 Click the Preview button next to the audio URL field
  • 2 An audio player appears below the field
  • 3 Click play — the teaching should start from the beginning
  • 4 If it plays correctly, you are ready to publish
⚠️ If the audio does not play or tries to download instead, the Google Drive file permissions are not set correctly. Go back to Drive → right-click the file → Share → confirm "Anyone with the link" is selected.
8
Publish and save
Makes the lesson visible to drivers
  • 1 Scroll down to the Publish section
  • 2 Toggle Make this lesson live to ON — the toggle turns green
  • 3 Click Save Changes
  • 4 A confirmation appears at the bottom of the screen
  • 5 The week in the sidebar now shows a green dot
💡 You can save a lesson with Publish OFF and come back to it later. Drivers will not see it until you turn Publish ON.
9
Test it in the app
Confirm your drivers can find it
  • 1 Open your ministry app URL in a new tab
  • 2 Type or speak a query related to this week's teaching — use a keyword you added, not the title
  • 3 The lesson card should appear with the title, scripture, summary, and audio player
  • 4 Press play — audio should start from the beginning
  • 5 If it works — you are done. Repeat for the next week.
💡 Test with emotional language, not titles. Type "I feel lonely" not "Week 7". That is how your drivers will actually query the chatbot at 2am.
